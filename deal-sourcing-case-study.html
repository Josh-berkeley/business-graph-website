Deal Sourcing Intelligence:
Meridian Software Discovery

How Cornerstone Capital leveraged 18-dimensional classification to identify Meridian Software 18 months before Series B, using systematic universe generation to spot supply chain automation convergence.

The Challenge

Traditional deal sourcing methods were failing to identify emerging opportunities in the supply chain automation space.

Cornerstone Capital's Deal Sourcing Problem (Q2 2022)

With $2.3B in AUM and focus on B2B software, Cornerstone's traditional sourcing methods were yielding obvious targets already tracked by 15+ competitors. The team needed to identify breakout companies 12-18 months before they became "hot deals" in the market.


Specific Pain Points:

  • NAICS code searches returned 200+ irrelevant logistics companies
  • Conference sourcing yielded companies already in 3-4 funding processes
  • Network referrals focused on obvious Series B/C targets with high valuations
  • No systematic way to identify supply chain + automation convergence themes

18-Dimensional Analysis Process

Our systematic approach to identifying Meridian Software through multi-dimensional classification.

1

Universe Definition

Define target universe using 18-dimensional filters instead of traditional industry codes. Focus on "supply chain software" + "automation capabilities" convergence.

2

Pattern Recognition

Identify companies with similar dimensional profiles to recent breakout successes. Look for early indicators of automation adoption in logistics software.

3

Signal Validation

Validate findings with public data: SEC Form-D filings, hiring patterns, product releases, and customer wins to confirm breakout potential.

4

Scoring & Ranking

Apply proprietary scoring algorithm across all 18 dimensions with confidence intervals. Rank opportunities by breakout probability and strategic fit.
